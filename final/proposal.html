Summary

Our project’s goal is to explore an efficient way to simulate flocking behavior with complex particle systems, particularly to visualize starling murmurations. Our project will involve building a mobile app in which users can select flocking related parameters, tap the screen to direct the birds, and view the corresponding simulations.

Problem Description

The problem our group is trying to solve is finding an efficient way to simulate generic particle flocking behavior and apply it directly to starling murmurations. We will be creating an interactive mobile app where users can select parameters related to the flock and direct where the flock should fly by tapping the screen. We believe this will be a great way to create quick and easy animations of birds that can be placed onto any scene an animator wants. This work can extend beyond a bird simulation, from modeling the behavior of other animals (fish, ants, etc.) that exhibit similar behavior to modeling new synthetic materials scientists have been designing that contain particles which move in a flocking pattern. This problem can get quite complex due to the many different variables involved with the motion of each bird as well as the number of birds that we will have to animate. At the same time, there are pairwise forces among all the birds that we will have to account for, and that can get computationally heavy. In terms of understanding the physics and variables that affect these flocks, there are many papers around the topic that we can refer to. In terms of speeding up calculations and making them less resource intensive, we plan on possibly taking advantage of spatially-partitioned bounding boxes or other methods suggested by papers. As a stretch goal, we can also try to speed up our implementation by using the GPU.

Goals and Deliverables

(1) Plan to Deliver

We plan on creating a mobile app from scratch that anyone can use to select information about the flock of birds they want to simulate (such as size, number of birds, etc.). Then, based on where the user taps on the screen, the flock of birds will move as a group towards the selected location. The user can keep tapping in different locations to create an entire scene, where they can simply direct key spots and the birds will automatically follow in a realistic manner.

One of our concerns is related to the efficiency of our simulation, so we will also be making sure to ensure there is no lag in between frames. For this, we may have to optimize the force calculations between the birds to be spatially localized. We can measure the speedups we get from any of these optimizations we implement.

(2) Hope to Deliver

If we get ahead of schedule, we would hope to also use the GPU to further speed up our calculations and run a smoother simulation. We will be able to quantify the results of this by recording the time our original solution takes and comparing it to the time GPU powered version takes.
